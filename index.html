Truth Lab

There is widespread confusion and misunderstanding about artificial intelligence among people. Many preconceived notions and cognitive biases are prevalent. For instance, learners often have a limited understanding of the technical principles of AI, and tend to anthropomorphize human characteristics and attributes onto AI systems, lacking a comprehensive understanding of their application scope, actual capabilities, and boundary limitations. [13]At the same time, when they view the risks, threats, and value of AI, they often fall into a binary opposition of black and white thinking, with ambiguous and unobjective viewpoints.

Future Strategies

To address the governance challenges brought about by issues such as AI hallucinations and Deepfake forgeries, it is necessary to establish a tripartite collaborative governance system integrating technology, law and society, achieving full-chain coverage of pre-event prevention, in-event supervision and post-event accountability, resolving public misconceptions about AI, and promoting the healthy and standardized development of AI technology.

Classifying Misinformation

How AI generates different categories of deceptive content, from deepfakes to hallucinated facts.

Wardle and Derakhshan (2017) put forward a three-dimensional framework for Information Disorder in the report of the Council of the European Union. The report emphasizes that the word "fake news" should be avoided because it does not accurately describe the complexity of information pollution.[4]

Mis-information

Sharing false information without intention of creating harm.

Dis-information

Knowing that it is false and still deliberately sharing to cause harm.

Mal-information

Sharing real information to cause harm (such as leaking privacy).

In the era of AIGC's increasing development, the boundaries of these three types of information have gradually become blurred, and the false content generated by AI is becoming more and more real, which has a strong impact on the traditional social credit judgment method.

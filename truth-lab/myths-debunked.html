Myths Debunked

Separating widespread misconceptions about AI from technical reality.

Myth One

AI tools are neutral, objective and impartial.

People often think that AI tools are "essentially just computers", relying solely on algorithms to process data, and thus their output is taken for granted as neutral and objective, free from the influence of stereotypes and preconceived notions. However, in reality, the content generated by AI can inherit and amplify injustice and bias from training data, and is also affected by factors such as system prompts and parameter settings.[14]

Their decision-making processes are usually not explainable, may give different results for the same prompt, and can be deliberately manipulated by human intervention.

Myth Two

Artificial intelligence tools think and learn like humans.

The terminology used in the field of artificial intelligence often employs terms such as "thinking", "intelligence", "training", "learning", and "hallucination", which are derived from human or biological domains. This can lead people to mistakenly believe that its operating mechanism is similar to that of humans (or the brain). However, in reality, artificial intelligence does not possess human-like intelligence and learning capabilities, nor does it have internal experiences such as emotions and life experiences; it does not "think" in the human way, and lacks the real feedback loop that shapes the human learning process.[15]

Myth Three

AI tools have empathy.

When the output of AI tools can soothe, identify with, or emotionally touch us, we may mistakenly believe that they "understand" themselves, and thus wrongly assume that the tools themselves possess emotions and empathy. Although AI can trigger strong emotional responses in humans, this does not mean that the tools themselves have genuine emotions; even "emotional computing" merely focuses on recognizing and simulating emotions, rather than truly generating emotions.[16]

This anthropomorphic perception makes empathy seem to exist, but in reality, it is just an illusion.

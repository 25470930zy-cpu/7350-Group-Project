Legal & Policy Countermeasures

The single-entity supervision is difficult to adapt to the cross-domain dissemination characteristics of AI technology.

Collaborative Supervision

A hierarchical and classified supervision framework of "government supervision, platform self-discipline, developers' responsibility, and users' compliance" needs to be established. The boundaries of the rights and responsibilities of each participating entity should be clarified, and a multi-party collaborative and full-process coverage supervision pattern should be formed. This will curb the generation and dissemination of AI false information at the institutional level.[19]

First

Content Labeling

Strengthen the regulation of content labeling, requiring AIGC content to be labeled both visibly and invisibly. Combined with digital watermarks, it can achieve traceability, helping the public distinguish between AI-generated and human-created content.

Second

Platform Obligations

Clarify the regulatory obligations of platforms, establish a rapid response and reporting mechanism for AI false information, and improve the handling efficiency.

Third

Accountability

Improve the accountability mechanism, hold illegal developers and malicious disseminators legally accountable, and form an effective deterrent.

Psychology and Trust

How AI heuristics drive both excessive trust and excessive doubt — and what each means for society.

Under the background of the increasing penetration of the public information ecology generated by artificial intelligence, excessive trust and excessive doubt, as two extreme manifestations of "AI heuristic", jointly erode the foundation of social trust. Research shows that users may have irrational excessive trust in AI-generated content, that is, that machine output is more objective; it may also cause excessive suspicion of overall denial due to lack of understanding of technology.[8] Both reactions are based on heuristic judgment rather than a careful assessment of the content of the message.

Over-trust

From the perspective of the long-term health of the trust foundation of society as a whole, overtrust may pose a more fundamental threat. When the public abandons critical verification because of the superficial fluency and authority of AI content, it substantially gives up the right to judge. This blind acceptance will open the floodgates for the large-scale spread of false information and disintegrate the factual basis of public discussion from within.[9] More seriously, excessive trust may lead to "cognitive unloading", which will cause the individual's information discrimination ability to deteriorate continuously.

May lead to "cognitive unloading" — continuous deterioration of individual information discrimination ability.

Over-doubt

Although excessive doubt may hinder the performance of technological benefits, it at least retains the possibility of questioning and verification. If this defensive attitude leads to more prudent assessment and stricter technical ethical requirements, it may become a positive force to promote governance. However, if it evolves into a total rejection of all digital information, it may give rise to a sense of "reality collapse" and exacerbate social division.

Risk: total rejection of all digital information may give rise to a sense of "reality collapse" and exacerbate social division.
